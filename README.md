# Embeddings & Vector Search

**Founders and Coders Open Workshop**

## Overview

This workshop will give you a flavour of our new AI engineering programme and covers embeddings—the foundational idea behind how modern AI represents meaning—and how to put them to practical use with vector search.

## Background

Embeddings are one of the most important ideas in modern AI. Every time a language model processes a sentence, every time a search engine returns results by relevance rather than keyword matching, every time a recommendation system finds something similar — embeddings are doing the work underneath.

The idea has deep roots in linguistics, but it was Word2Vec in 2013 that made it practical: a method for learning vector representations of words from raw text. The technique is elegant, the results are powerful, and the core insight generalises far beyond words to sentences, documents, images, and more.

Once you understand embeddings, a whole class of AI applications opens up. Vector databases let you store and search over millions of them. Cosine similarity lets you measure how closely related two vectors are. Chunking and embedding a set of documents turns any corpus into something searchable without keyword matching.

During this workshop, we will start by building a real intuition for how embeddings are learned and what they capture. Then we will put them to use: you will build a working semantic search system over real documents.
